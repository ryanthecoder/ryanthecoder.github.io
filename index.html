Integrating multiple home manufacturing devices to form more increase complexity of automated construction

 

Three dimensional printers have taken off in the past three years, and home models are now able to make intricate and interesting designs. As professional- and consumer-grade versions increase in popularity, extensibility will need to be addressed. This project explores new technologies in 3D printing and is broken up into three phases: research, machine engineering, and 3D GCode development.

The 3D printer extension machine is based of the Shapeoko machine but features several modifications the most important of which is replace the routing tool with a arm capable of holding several tools and extending them into the 3d printer. The development of this machine is ongoing and is tracked here. Development has begun with the Makerbot Replicator 2 but will include other 3D printers in the future. Here is a demo of the two machines functioning together.

It is the hope of this project to have several tools available for the extension machine, but for the initial phase of development I will be using an electromagnet to pick up and embed pieces into the 3D print at some part in the middle of a print. Embedding functional parts into the plastic of a 3D Print will greatly extend the ability of 3D printers and is fairly easy to implement.

 

I have forked several open source software packages that I will adapt for this project. You are invited to follow me on my github.
